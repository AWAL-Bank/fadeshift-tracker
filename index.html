2. Regime Narrative — 3-Act Structure

Act 1 Feb 3 – Feb 22

The Bullish Consensus

Most assets started the period in bullish territory as the Oil Shock consensus formed and banks piled into commodities, equities, and EM. Gold and EM Equities both hit +0.90. The Fed consensus started dovish before flipping hawkish as the PCE/inflation narrative took hold.

Act 2 Feb 23 – Mar 14

The Hormuz Shock & First Fracture

The Strait of Hormuz disruption triggered the first wave of de-risking. EM Equities and S&P 500 collapsed while commodities initially held up. The critical fracture appeared in Gold as surging real yields undermined the safe-haven thesis. Citi was the first bank to flip Gold bearish (Mar 12) — the leading signal that the traditional playbook was breaking.

Act 3 Mar 15 – Mar 23

The Everything Sell-Off

The stagflationary regime triggered a broad liquidation across equities, credit, FX, and commodities simultaneously. Brent reversed −0.92 from peak. Credit IG reversed −0.92. EUR/USD and Gold each reversed −0.58. The ECB hike consensus weakened −0.50 from its perfect +1.00 starting point.
